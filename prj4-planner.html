Overview

During a high-level interface design course, my teammate and I had two weeks to design an additional feature for an existing app called Citymapper. Citymapper is a transportation app that provides different methods to get around the city without driving. Citymapper’s goal is to manage your life in the city with everything you need and may not even realize you need. We designed a planner feature for Citymapper that allows users to save and schedule transit routes ahead of time.

My Roles: User Research / Experience Design / Interface Design

Tools: Figma

The Problem

From our research, we found that people mostly use transit apps to plan their trips ahead by checking transit timetables to ensure they don’t miss their trains/bus, and people usually find it frustrating for having to search for different schedules while they need to transfer between multiple trains/bus.

Target Users

The app is mainly used by individuals that live within the city or traveling in another city

The Goal

- To allow users to plan trips ahead of time

- Quick access to transit schedules

- Locate all relative schedules in one area within the app

- Enable reminders for coming up scheduled trips

Feature Walkthrough

How a user might make use of this feature

- This planner reminds the user to begin departure based on the saved bus/train’s location

- Someone that uses the same route every day, may use the planner feature to accommodate for the unreliability of transit arrivals

- Somebody who needs to take multiple different routes within a day may use this feature to help them keep track on schedule

Project Takeaways

Citymapper had a lot of mature features already, but after talking with some friends that rely on transit apps regularly and testing Citymapper with them, there were still minor but important user goals that the app could not achieve. So my takeaway is that the quality of a feature is more important than the quantity. Doing user interviews and knowing what they truly need is always essential when being a user experience designer
